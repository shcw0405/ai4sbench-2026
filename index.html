About

AI-for-Science (AI4S) has emerged as a rapidly growing research area, driven by the availability of large-scale scientific datasets and advances in AI technologies. These methods are increasingly applied to scientific tasks such as protein structure prediction, single-cell genomics, and climate and Earth system modeling. Their effectiveness critically depends on the quality and AI-readiness of scientific datasets, as well as the performance, robustness, and reliability of AI models. Systematic evaluation of data, models, and applications is essential to ensure reproducible and trustworthy scientific discoveries.

AI4S-Bench aims to foster research on AI4S benchmarking by promoting the development of standardized datasets, evaluation protocols, and metrics, while encouraging reproducibility, transparency, and FAIR principles across diverse scientific domains. By providing a forum for discussion and collaboration, AI4S-Bench aligns with ICDM's mission of advancing knowledge discovery and data mining.

Topics We Aim to Address

Scientific Data Evaluation
Methods for assessing data quality and AI readiness; standardized metrics and protocols for scientific datasets; design, curation, and application of benchmark datasets.
AI Models and Agents for Science
Task-specific evaluation for scientific models; assessing the performance, reliability, robustness, interpretability, and safety of large language models and agentic AI on scientific tasks; standardized benchmarks across scientific domains.
AI4S for Scientific Discovery
Evaluating AI-assisted workflows, hypothesis generation, and knowledge discovery; assessing AI-driven decision support and experimental design in scientific research.
Standards and Best Practices
Establishing community-wide guidelines for reproducible benchmarking of datasets, models, and AI4S methods; promoting transparency, FAIR principles, and consistent reporting of AI performance in science.

Call for Papers

The 1st International Workshop on AI-for-Science Benchmarking (AI4S-Bench) @ ICDM 2026 invites submissions on all aspects of benchmarking AI for science, including datasets, models, evaluation methodologies, and scientific discovery. Scope

We welcome contributions across a broad spectrum of topics, including but not limited to:

  • Methods for assessing data quality and AI readiness of scientific datasets
  • Standardized metrics, evaluation protocols, and benchmark datasets across scientific domains
  • Task-specific evaluation for scientific models (e.g., protein structure, single-cell genomics, climate modeling)
  • Assessing performance, reliability, robustness, and safety of LLMs and agentic AI on scientific tasks
  • Interpretability, transparency, and trustworthiness of AI in scientific research
  • Standardized benchmarks and evaluation frameworks for cross-domain comparison
  • Evaluating AI-assisted workflows, hypothesis generation, and knowledge discovery
  • Assessing AI-driven decision support and experimental design in scientific research
  • Community-wide guidelines for reproducible benchmarking and FAIR principles in AI4S

Submission Guidelines

Format:  All submissions must be a single PDF file, formatted according to the latest IEEE Conference Proceedings Template. We welcome high-quality original papers in the following two tracks:

  • Regular Research Papers: 8 pages (including all content and references)
  • Vision Papers and Short Technical Papers: 4–6 pages (including all content and references)

Review Criteria:   Submitted papers will be assessed based on novelty, technical quality, potential impact, insightfulness, depth, clarity, and reproducibility.

Best Paper Award:   A Best Paper Award will be presented to recognize one outstanding submission.

Contact:   For any questions, please contact us at ai4sbench2026@easychair.org.
